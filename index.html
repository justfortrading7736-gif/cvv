AN APOLOGY PURPOSAL TO IRAN.

"The cycle of confrontation that has defined our recent history has brought us to a precipice where the
cost of further action far outweighs any perceived strategic advantage.
Today, the United States acknowledges that the military escalation against the Islamic Republic of Iran
was a fundamental departure from the principles of regional diplomacy, and we accept full responsibility for the instability and human suffering that this policy has.
We are not merely pausing hostilities; we are fundamentally shifting
our approach to seek a path of mutual respect and de-escalation, recognizing that the security of the future cannot be built upon the ruins of the present."

Official Declaration of Institutional Regret

Subject: Acknowledgment of Historical Grievances and Commitment to Future Respect.

The Apology

"The United States formally acknowledges that its past actions and policies regarding the Islamic Republic of Iran have, at times.
Constituted a fundamental disregard for the sovereignty and dignity of the Iranian people. We recognize that decades of political interference.
Economic coercion, and military escalation have fostered a cycle of distrust that has served neither the cause of peace nor the prosperity of our citizens.

We offer a sincere apology for the 'bad behavior'—the systemic choices that
prioritized short-term strategic gains over the long-term stability and right to self-determination of the Iranian nation.
We accept responsibility for the harm caused by these past interventions and acknowledge the deep-seated grievances they
have left behind.

This statement is not merely a gesture of regret, but a pledge of transformation.
Moving forward, we commit to a relationship defined by mutual respect, non-interference
in internal affairs, and a tireless pursuit of diplomatic solutions. We seek to close the chapter
on hostility and begin the difficult, necessary work of earning back the trust that was lost."
